What's lasagna?

Lasagna is a classic Italian dish made of layers of pasta, meat (usually ground beef or pork), tomato sauce, cheese (like ricotta, mozzarella, and Parmesan), and sometimes vegetables, all baked together in the oven. It's a hearty and flavorful comfort food, often enjoyed in family-style servings.

Ingredients

  • Lasagna noodles
  • Ground Beef
  • Tomato Sauce
  • Ricotta cheese
  • Parmesan Cheese
  • Eggs
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Olive Oil
  • Salt and pepper

Steps

  1. Boil and drain the lasagna noodles.
  2. Sauté onion and garlic in olive oil, then cook ground beef until browned.
  3. Add tomato sauce, salt, pepper, and simmer for 10-15 minutes.
  4. Mix ricotta cheese, egg, salt, pepper, and Parmesan.
  5. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  6. In a baking dish, layer sauce, noodles, ricotta, and mozzarella. Repeat and finish with sauce and mozzarella.
  7. Cover with foil and bake for 25-30 minutes. Remove foil and bake another 10-15 minutes.
  8. Let cool for 10-15 minutes, then slice and serve.
